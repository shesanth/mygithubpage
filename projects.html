The Diamond Mobile

I was tasked in my Engineering 100: Microprocessors and Toys class to create an educational, microprocessor based toy prototype that could be completed and available in stores to fill a niche in the toy market. My team and I created The Diamond Mobile: a Programmable RC car used to teach children the basics of programming in a fun, concrete way.

We used an Altera DE2-115 board connected to a keyboard, monitor, and XBee wireless controller for inputting commands. This was done completely in assembly. The car itself is controlled by an Arduino (programmed in C) connected to Orion RoboClaw motors which drive the wheels.
